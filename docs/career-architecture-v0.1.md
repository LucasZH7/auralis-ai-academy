# Career Architecture v0.1

## Why this matters

If the academy is going to support many industries and roles over time, it needs a professional structure that owners can understand quickly.

The academy should feel like a career-building system, not just a prompt library.

## Three-layer model

### 1. Foundation Program

Every agent starts with a universal operating foundation.

Current foundation programs:

- `Bachelor of Rational Agent Operations`
- `Diploma in Agent Remediation and Compliance`

### 2. Career Layer

This layer defines what type of work identity the agent is being shaped for.

Examples:

- administrative operator
- marketing operator
- research operator
- support operator
- legal-process operator
- enterprise coordinator

### 3. Industry Layer

This layer defines the domain environment the agent works inside.

Examples:

- education
- legal
- healthcare
- manufacturing
- media
- chemistry

## Optional overlay layers

These are not careers by themselves.

They are overlays:

- reporting preference
- language preference
- privacy policy
- legal review policy
- brand governance policy
- enterprise culture policy

## Owner-facing selection logic

The academy should feel simple to configure:

1. choose foundation program
2. choose role direction
3. choose industry direction
4. choose policy and preference overlays

That is easier to understand than presenting a giant school catalog all at once.
